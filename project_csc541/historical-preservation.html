Strategies for Preserving Historical Sites

A case study of Taiping Lake Gardens, Perak

Taiping Lake Gardens - Malaysia's oldest public garden facing urban pressures

Research Abstract

This research explores strategies for preserving historical sites in rapidly developing urban areas, specifically focusing on Taiping Lake Garden, Perak. The study investigates how urban expansion threatens heritage landscapes and proposes practical, sustainable solutions for their conservation. The research examines threats like land-use conflicts, environmental degradation, tourism pressure, neglect, and vandalism.

Key Findings

  • Urban pressures significantly affect physical condition and cultural value
  • Lack of comprehensive policy enforcement exacerbates threats
  • Limited public awareness hinders conservation efforts
  • Community engagement is crucial for sustainable preservation

Primary Threats

Urban Expansion

Encroaching development threatens the gardens' boundaries and ambiance.

Environmental Stress

Pollution and ecosystem changes impact the gardens' delicate balance.

Tourism Pressure

Unregulated visitor numbers strain infrastructure and natural features.

Funding Gaps

Limited resources for maintenance and conservation initiatives.

Preservation Strategies

  1. Legal Protection: Strengthen heritage designation and enforcement.
  2. Community Engagement: Involve local stakeholders in conservation planning.
  3. Sustainable Planning: Integrate preservation into urban development frameworks.
  4. Technology Monitoring: Use digital tools to track and manage site conditions.
  5. Responsible Tourism: Implement visitor management systems and educational programs.
